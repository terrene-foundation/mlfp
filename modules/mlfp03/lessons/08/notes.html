Slides 3-4~6 minDataFlow

DataFlow: zero-config persistence

Slide 3: Walk the model definition and create operation. "Decorate a class with @db.model and DataFlow generates the database table and CRUD operations. No SQL, no ORM configuration."

Slide 4: Walk the read, update, delete operations. "This is how you persist predictions in production. Every prediction is timestamped and versioned so you can audit later."

Note on async: "The await keyword means the operation runs asynchronously — the program can do other work while waiting for the database. For now, just use it; we will explain async fully in the Advanced Certificate."
→ "Drift monitoring."
Slides 5-7~10 minDriftMonitor

DriftMonitor: PSI and KS

Slide 5: Walk the PSI formula. "PSI bins the feature values and compares the proportions between reference and current. A PSI above 0.25 means the distributions have shifted significantly."

Slide 6: Walk the KS test diagram. "The KS test finds the maximum vertical distance between two CDFs. The further apart they are, the more the distribution has shifted."

Slide 7: Walk the code. "You define a DriftSpec with features and thresholds, create a DriftMonitor, and call check(). The report tells you which features have drifted and by how much."

For experts: "PSI is symmetric and always non-negative. KS gives a p-value but can be overpowered on large datasets. In practice, use both: PSI for the magnitude of shift, KS for statistical significance."
→ "Prediction uncertainty."
Slide 8~5 minConformal prediction

Conformal prediction

"Most models give a point prediction: 'the price is $450,000'. Conformal prediction adds an interval: '$420,000 to $480,000 with 90% coverage'. The interval is distribution-free — no Normal assumption, no parametric model."

Walk the code. "Compute residuals on a calibration set. Take the 90th percentile. That is your interval width. Three lines of code."

If beginners are confused by 'distribution-free': "It means the interval works regardless of whether your errors are Normal, skewed, or any other shape. That is rare and powerful."
