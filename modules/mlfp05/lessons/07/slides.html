Transfer Learning

ResNet fine-tuning · BERT fine-tuning · ONNX export · InferenceServer

⏱ 90 minutes · Outcome: fine-tune, export, and serve CV and NLP models

Why transfer learning?

  • Training from scratch: millions of images, weeks of compute.
  • Transfer: start from pre-trained weights, fine-tune on your data.
  • Works because early layers learn universal features (edges, syntax).
🟢 Foundations: most production DL does NOT train from scratch. Transfer is the norm.

Lower layers transfer; higher layers specialize

The strategy

ResNet fine-tuning


model = models.resnet18(weights=models.ResNet18_Weights.DEFAULT)
for param in model.parameters():
    param.requires_grad = False  # freeze

model.fc = torch.nn.Linear(
    model.fc.in_features, num_classes)  # new head
optimizer = torch.optim.Adam(
    model.fc.parameters(), lr=1e-3)

Common mistakes

  1. Training all layers from start — freeze first, unfreeze gradually.
  2. LR too high — use 10–100x lower than from-scratch training.
  3. No augmentation — small datasets need flips, rotations, jitter.
  4. No ONNX export — notebook-only model is not deployed.
