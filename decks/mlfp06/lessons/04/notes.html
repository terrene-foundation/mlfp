Speaker Notes — Lesson 6.4RAG Systems

Total time: ~90 minutes  ·  12 slides  ·  ~7–8 min per slide

RAG is the most immediately deployable technique in M6. Most students will build their first RAG system within a week of this lesson. Spend 30% of time on live demo (building the pipeline), 30% on retrieval comparison (BM25 vs dense vs hybrid), and 20% on RAGAS evaluation. HyDE is the bonus — show it as an easy win.

Slide 1~2 minTitle

RAG Systems

Frame: "Your LLM is smart but it has two fatal flaws: it does not know what happened after its training cutoff, and it does not know your company's internal documents. RAG fixes both: fetch relevant facts from a search index and inject them into the prompt. By the end of this lesson you will have a working RAG pipeline on Singapore regulatory documents."

→ "Why RAG beats fine-tuning for knowledge."
Slide 2~5 minWhy RAG

Why RAG beats fine-tuning

Walk the three reasons. Freshness resonates most: "MAS issued new crypto regulations last month. Do you want to retrain your LLM? Or just update the index? Indexing takes minutes. Retraining takes days and thousands of dollars." Attribution is the compliance argument: "In financial services, you need to cite your sources. RAG gives you the exact chunk that produced the answer. Fine-tuning gives you weights."

→ "The pipeline."
Slides 3–5~15 minPipeline, chunking, retrieval

Pipeline, chunking, and retrieval

Walk the pipeline diagram end to end. For chunking: "200–500 tokens is the sweet spot. Always overlap by 10–20% so you do not split a key passage at a chunk boundary." For retrieval: "BM25 is great for exact keyword matches — if the user types 'PDPA Section 24', BM25 finds it. Dense retrieval is great for semantic matches — 'data privacy rules' matches 'PDPA' even though the words differ. Hybrid combines both. Production systems always use hybrid + re-ranking."

→ "How do we know it works?"
Slides 6–7~10 minRAGAS and HyDE

RAGAS and HyDE

Walk the four RAGAS metrics. "Faithfulness: is the answer grounded in context? Answer relevance: does it address the question? Context relevance: are the chunks relevant? Context recall: did we find all relevant chunks?" Then HyDE: "Generate a hypothetical answer with the LLM, embed that, use it for retrieval. Why does this work? Because the hypothetical answer looks like a real document, so it retrieves documents that look like answers rather than documents that share keywords with the question."

For beginners: "HyDE is like asking a friend who roughly knows the topic to describe what the answer might look like, then searching for documents that match that description."
→ "Code demo."
Slides 8–9~10 minCode and Singapore

Code demo and Singapore context

Walk the code line by line. Live demo if possible: load MAS documents, chunk them, embed with sentence-transformers, retrieve top-5, generate answer. The Singapore context is natural — MAS, PDPA, AI Verify are documents every professional in Singapore encounters.

→ "Mistakes."
Slides 10–12~8 minMistakes and recap

Mistakes and recap

Walk mistakes. #1 (chunks too large) is the most common: "If you pass 2000-token chunks to the LLM, most of each chunk is irrelevant noise. The LLM gets distracted and produces worse answers. Smaller chunks = more precise retrieval = better answers." Preview 6.5: "Next we give the LLM the ability to act. ReAct agents reason about a task, decide which tool to use, observe the result, and iterate."

→ End of lesson.
