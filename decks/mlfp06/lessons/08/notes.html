Speaker Notes — Lesson 6.8Capstone — Full Production Platform

Total time: ~120 minutes  ·  10 slides  ·  ~12 min per slide (integration demo)

This is the final lesson of the entire programme. The mood should be celebratory but technically rigorous. Students deploy a real system and test it end-to-end. The six-module journey table (Slide 8) is the emotional anchor — walk it slowly and let them feel the distance they have covered. The final slide is a celebration, not a teaser for more content.

Slide 1~5 minTitle

Capstone — Full Production Platform

Set the scene: "This is the last lesson. In Lesson 1.1, you wrote your first line of Python against Singapore weather data. Today, you deploy a governed, multi-channel AI system. Let's make it count." Take a moment to acknowledge how far they have come. Some started with zero coding experience.

→ "The complete stack."
Slides 2–3~15 minStack and Nexus

The stack and Nexus deployment

Walk the pipeline diagram: "Train a model with TrainingPipeline. Persist with DataFlow. Wrap in a Kaizen agent. Govern with PACT. Deploy with Nexus. Monitor with DriftMonitor. Six packages, one pipeline." Then the Nexus code: "Four lines: create the app, add auth, add rate limiting, register the service, serve. Three channels — API, CLI, MCP — from one codebase."

→ "Authentication."
Slides 4–5~15 minRBAC and DriftMonitor

Auth and monitoring

Walk the role table: "Admin deploys. Analyst queries. Viewer reads. Unauthenticated → 401. Wrong role → 403." Then DriftMonitor: "You trained the model on 2024 data. It is now 2026. The distribution has shifted. DriftMonitor detects this and alerts your team before the predictions go stale."

→ "End-to-end verification."
Slide 6~15 minE2E test

End-to-end verification

Walk the three tests. "Test 1: authenticated request succeeds with a prediction. Test 2: unauthenticated request is rejected with 401. Test 3: analyst trying to access admin endpoint is rejected with 403. These three tests prove the system works, the auth works, and the governance works."

If anyone asks 'why test this?': "Because untested deployment is a liability. In Singapore's regulated environment (MAS, PDPA), you need to demonstrate that your system rejects unauthorised access. These tests are your evidence."
→ "The journey."
Slides 7–8~10 minJourney table

The six-module journey

Walk the table slowly. Pause at each module. "M1: you could not write Python. M2: you understood statistics. M3: you built production ML pipelines. M4: you discovered the pivot — optimisation creates features. M5: you implemented every DL architecture. M6: you deployed a governed AI system." Let the weight of the journey sink in.

→ "What you can do."
Slides 9–10~10 minRecap and celebration

Congratulations

Run through the final checklist. Then the celebration: "From print('Hello') to production ML platform. Six modules. 48 lessons. Zero to practitioner." Pause. Let them feel it. "The Kailash platform is your toolkit. Singapore is your testing ground. Go build something that matters."

Close with: "Take five minutes to write down one thing you want to build with what you have learned. Share it with your neighbour. That is your next project."

→ End of the MLFP programme.
