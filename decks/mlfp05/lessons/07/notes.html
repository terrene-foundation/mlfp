Speaker Notes — Lesson 5.7Transfer Learning

Total time: ~90 minutes  ·  9 slides  ·  ~10 min per slide (more code demos)

This is the most practical lesson in M5 — students leave with a deployed model. Allocate 40% of time to live demos (ResNet fine-tuning, BERT fine-tuning, ONNX export). The architecture selection guide (Slide 7) is the reference table for the rest of the course. Emphasise that this IS how production ML works — almost nobody trains from scratch.

Slide 1~3 minTitle

Transfer Learning

Frame: "This is the most immediately useful lesson in M5. By the end you will have a fine-tuned model exported to ONNX and served by InferenceServer. This pipeline — pre-train, fine-tune, export, serve — is how 90% of production DL systems work."

→ "Why it works."
Slide 2~5 minWhy

Why transfer learning

"ResNet was trained on 14 million ImageNet images. BERT was trained on 3.3 billion tokens of English text. You do not have that data or that compute. But the features those models learned — edges, textures, syntax, semantics — are universal. Transfer learning lets you stand on the shoulders of those billion-dollar training runs."

For beginners: "Imagine learning to play guitar. If you already play piano, your fingers know music theory, timing, and dexterity. You transfer those skills. Transfer learning is the same idea for neural networks."
→ "The strategy."
Slide 3~5 minStrategy

Freeze-tune-head

Walk the three-box diagram. "Blue: early layers that detect edges and textures — frozen, never touched. Yellow: later layers that detect shapes and patterns — fine-tuned with a low learning rate. Green: your new classification head — trained from scratch. This layered unfreezing preserves universal knowledge while adapting to your task."

→ "CV example: ResNet."
Slides 4–5~20 minCode demos

ResNet and BERT fine-tuning

Live demo if possible. Walk each code block line by line. For ResNet: "Load pretrained, freeze, replace final FC layer, train only the new layer." For BERT: "Load from HuggingFace, the Trainer handles everything — you just provide data and training arguments." Emphasise the simplicity — 5 lines for ResNet, 4 lines for BERT.

→ "Export and serve."
Slide 6~10 minONNX + InferenceServer

ONNX export + serving

Walk the three-line pipeline. "OnnxBridge.export takes your model, a sample input, and an output path. InferenceServer loads the ONNX model, warm_cache prepares it for fast inference, predict serves predictions. From Jupyter notebook to production API in three lines. This is the Kailash deployment story."

→ "Selection guide."
Slide 7~5 minSelection guide

Architecture selection guide

Walk the table. "Images: always transfer (ImageNet). Text: always transfer (BERT/GPT). Sequences: sometimes transfer if the domain matches. Graphs: rarely — too task-specific. Tabular: never — gradient boosting still wins, no useful pre-training." Make them photograph this table.

→ "Mistakes."
Slides 8–9~8 minMistakes + recap

Mistakes and recap

Walk mistakes. #2 (LR too high) is the most common — "Pre-trained weights encode billions of training examples. A high LR destroys that in one epoch. Use 1e-5 for BERT, 1e-4 for ResNet fine-tuning." Preview 5.8: "Reinforcement Learning — the final DL paradigm. Learning from interaction rather than static data. Then we move to M6: LLMs and production systems."

→ End of lesson.
