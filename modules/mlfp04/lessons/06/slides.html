NLP — From Text to Topics

TF-IDF · BM25 · Word2Vec · LDA · BERTopic · NPMI coherence

⏱ 75 minutes   ·   Outcome: extract and evaluate topics from Singapore news

Text: the most common unstructured data

  • Support tickets, reviews, news articles, legal contracts, chat logs.
  • Nobody reads them all — topic modelling discovers themes automatically.
  • Themes become features for downstream classification and routing.
🟢 Foundations: same discovery mindset as clustering and association rules — but the "items" are words.

Text analysis pipeline

TF-IDF — weighting words by importance

\[ \text{tfidf}(t, d) \;=\; \text{tf}(t, d) \cdot \log \frac{N}{\text{df}(t)} \]
  • tf = how often does the word appear in this document?
  • idf = how rare is the word across all documents?
  • Common words (the, and) get idf ≈ 0 → contribute nothing.
🟢 Foundations: think of idf as a "surprise" weight. Common words are not surprising; rare words are.

BM25 — TF-IDF's smarter cousin

\[ \text{BM25}(t,d) \;=\; \text{idf}(t) \cdot \frac{(k_1+1)\,\text{tf}(t,d)}{\text{tf}(t,d)+k_1\!\left(1-b+b\,\frac{|d|}{\text{avg}|d|}\right)} \]
  • TF-IDF is linear in term frequency — 10 repeats = 10x weight. BM25 saturates.
  • Longer documents penalised via length normalisation ($b \approx 0.75$).
  • Powers Elasticsearch, Lucene, and every search engine you have used.

LDA — the generative story

  1. For each topic $k$: draw word distribution $\phi_k$ from Dirichlet($\beta$).
  2. For each document $d$: draw topic mixture $\theta_d$ from Dirichlet($\alpha$).
  3. For each word position: draw topic $z$ from $\theta_d$, then draw word from $\phi_z$.

Inference reverses the story: given words, find the most likely topics.

🟢 Foundations: LDA is soft clustering for documents. Each document belongs to multiple topics, like GMM's soft assignments.

LDA plate notation

NMF — topics via matrix factorisation

Decompose the TF-IDF matrix $X \approx W H$ with non-negative entries.

  • $W$ rows = topic weights per document.
  • $H$ rows = word weights per topic.
  • Faster than LDA, sparser topics, more interpretable.
🔵 Theory: NMF is the closest relative of PCA (Lesson 4.3). Same idea: low-rank factorisation, different constraint (non-negativity).

BERTopic — the 2020s upgrade

  • Captures semantic relationships, not just co-occurrence.
  • Auto-determines number of topics.
  • Needs a few hundred documents minimum.

BERTopic in practice

from sentence_transformers import SentenceTransformer

NPMI — evaluating topic quality

\[ \text{NPMI}(w_i, w_j) \;=\; \frac{\log P(w_i, w_j) - \log\bigl(P(w_i)\,P(w_j)\bigr)}{-\log P(w_i, w_j)} \]
  • Range $[-1, 1]$: positive for words that co-occur more than chance.
  • Average over top-10 words per topic, then across all topics.
  • Higher is better. The standard metric in modern topic modelling papers.
⚠ Warning: LDA perplexity does NOT correlate with human topic quality. Use NPMI instead.

Four common mistakes

  1. Skipping stopword removal — top words: "the", "and", "to". Useless.
  2. Wrong K — start 10–20, tune with coherence.
  3. Evaluating on perplexity — does NOT reflect human quality judgments.
  4. BERTopic on tiny corpora — needs hundreds of docs. Fall back to NMF.
