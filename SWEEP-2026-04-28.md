## Findings

### CRIT-01 — Diagnostics out-of-the-box absent in M2/M3/M4

**[Sweep 5][CRIT]** `km.diagnose` is the entire reason students don't hand-write evaluation harnesses. M5 has it at every culminating-technique lesson (16 files). M2 (validation lessons), M3 (every classifier lesson), M4 (every clustering lesson) have ZERO `km.diagnose`.

- **Location**: `modules/mlfp02/`, `modules/mlfp03/solutions/ex_3..ex_8/`, `modules/mlfp04/solutions/ex_1..ex_5/`
- **Why this matters**: Students who only see `km.diagnose` in M5 perceive it as a "deep-learning thing." Diagnostics is universal — every model class benefits from `diagnose(model, kind='auto', data=val_set)`. The gold-standard pattern is the M5 lesson-tail cell at `modules/mlfp05/solutions/ex_1/11_grand_comparison.py`.
- **Disposition**: **FILE-ISSUE** — beyond shard budget (~24 files × 2 formats = ~48 edits, plus notebook regen). Recommend a dedicated `feat/m2-m4-diagnose-tails` branch.
- **Action taken**: none yet (deferred to next session per shard discipline).

### CRIT-02 — Engine-first km.train absent outside M5 ex_0

**[Sweep 5][CRIT]** `km.train` is the destination-first one-liner that anchors every progressive lesson ("here's what you just built; here's the engine that does it for you"). Only M5 ex_0 prelude has it (`modules/mlfp05/solutions/ex_0/00_destination_first.py`). M3 every-lesson-tail and M4 every-culminating-lesson lack the equivalent cell.

- **Location**: M3 ex_3 through ex_8 (5 files × 2 formats); M4 ex_1 through ex_5 (5 files × 2 formats); M3 ex_7 hp_search (the easiest lift — `km.train(hp_search='optuna', n_trials=50)` replaces ~30 LOC of primitive composition).
- **Why this matters**: Without the engine-first tail cell, students think the primitive composition (TrainingPipeline + HyperparameterSearch + ModelRegistry) IS the destination, when it's actually Layer-1 scaffolding under `km.train`. Loss of pedagogical anchor: "so this is what `km.train` does inside."
- **Disposition**: **FILE-ISSUE** — pair with CRIT-01 for one combined `feat/m3-m4-engine-first-tails` shard.

### CRIT-03 — `km.dashboard` never landed

**[Sweep 5][CRIT]** Each M5 lesson tracks to its own SQLite (mlfp05_autoencoders.db, mlfp05_cnns.db, mlfp05_rnns.db, etc.). `km.dashboard(store_url=...)` is the visual readout of every recorded experiment — without it, instructors have run-history rows but no dashboard. Confirmed unused: `grep -rEln 'km\.dashboard' modules/` returns 0.

- **Location**: M3 ex_8 wrap-up, M4 ex_5 wrap-up, M5 every lesson reflection cell, M5 final exam summary.
- **Why this matters**: kailash-ml 1.5.1 exports `km.dashboard` and `km.lineage` precisely so a one-liner emits the run-history visual. Without it, students close the loop manually (matplotlib over `tracker.list_runs()`).
- **Disposition**: **FILE-ISSUE** — small (~10 lesson-reflection cells) but pedagogically meaningful. Defer until each lesson has accumulated ≥10 runs (per session-notes Open Q1, this is a curriculum-pace decision the human should make).

## Cross-cutting observations

1. **The four properties are most coherent in M5 because M5 is the only module rebuilt after kailash-ml 1.0+ (engine-first surface) was stable.** M3 was authored against 0.x primitives. M4 against 0.x. The migration to 1.5.1 happened on the branch but didn't lift M1–M4 to engine-first; only M5 received the lift.

2. **The M5 ex_0 destination-first prelude is the canonical reference.** It demonstrates `km.device` + `km.train` + `km.MLEngine` + `km.register` in 150 LOC. Every subsequent module's "engine-first tail" cell should follow that pattern.

3. **Engine-first ≠ replacing primitive composition.** Per CLAUDE.md Directive 5 (Progressive Disclosure), M3 deliberately uses raw `.fit()` + `TrainingPipeline` to teach the underlying mechanics. The fix is **adding** a tail cell, not **replacing** the body. Same lesson, two views: primitive (top, pedagogy) → engine (tail, destination).

4. **M6 is correctly excluded from `km.*` work.** Per CLAUDE.md Module Map, M6 uses Kaizen + Align + PACT + Nexus + MCP, not km. The "engine-first" property in M6 means using `Delegate` + `BaseAgent` + `AlignmentPipeline` instead of raw OpenAI / TRL.

5. **AutoML coverage is the only property at appropriate density.** 2 sites (M3 exam, M4 ex_1/05), placed AFTER students master manual selection. More AutoML would dilute the "you can ALSO automate this" pedagogical beat.
